<h2>Accountancy Chapter 1 - Personal Notes</h2>

  <h3>Transaction</h3>
  <p><b>Definition:</b> A transaction is a transfer of a thing of value from one holder to another. <br>
  OR <br>
  A transaction is a value shift from one account to another that can be measured in monetary terms.</p>

  <h3>Types of Accounting</h3>
  <ol>
    <li><b>Financial Accounting:</b> Financial accounting is accounting which deals with all financial transactions of a business in order to report the business's financial health. <br>
    OR <br>
    Financial Accounting is the process of recording, summarizing, and reporting a business's financial transactions to provide an accurate picture of its financial health.</li>

    <li><b>Cost Accounting:</b> Cost accounting is the process of tracking, recording, analyzing, and controlling costs associated with the production/acquiring of goods or services.</li>

    <li><b>Management Accounting:</b> Management accounting is the process of collecting, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting financial and non-financial information to help managers make decisions, plan, control operations, and achieve business goals.</li>
  </ol>

  <h3>Objectives of Accounting</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>To maintain systematic records of business transactions</li>
    <li>To determine profit or loss</li>
    <li>To ascertain financial position</li>
    <li>To provide information to users</li>
    <li>To assist in decision making</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>Advantages of Accounting</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Helps in decision making</li>
    <li>Provides evidence in legal matters</li>
    <li>Helps in tax computation</li>
    <li>Helps in comparing business position</li>
    <li>Assists in obtaining loans</li>
    <li>Helps in detecting frauds and errors</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>Limitations of Accounting</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Based on historical data</li>
    <li>Ignores non-monetary factors</li>
    <li>Possibility of manipulation (window dressing)</li>
    <li>Affected by personal judgments</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>Qualitative Characteristics of Accounting</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Relevance:</b> Timeliness, Materiality</li>
    <li><b>Reliability:</b> Verifiability, Neutrality, Completeness</li>
    <li><b>Comparability:</b> Consistency, Standard Methods</li>
    <li><b>Understandability:</b> Clear Presentation and Format, Simple Language, Not too technical terms</li>
  </ul>
